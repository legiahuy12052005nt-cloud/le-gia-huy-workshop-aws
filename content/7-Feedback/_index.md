> This section reflects my personal insights and hands-on experiences during the 12-week First Cloud AI Journey, aimed at helping the FCAJ team continuously optimize the program for future cohorts.

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment** The learning and working environment at the AWS Vietnam office is exceptionally professional, dynamic, and collaborative. The open-space layout stimulates creativity and provides an ideal setting for interns to engage in high-intensity teamwork and optimize technical architectures.

**2. Support from Mentors / Team Admin** The mentors (AWS Solutions Architects) possess profound technical expertise, offering dedicated guidance while orienting our troubleshooting mindset toward AWS Well-Architected security principles. The administrative team is highly supportive, responsive, and generously provides ample AWS Credits, ensuring a flexible sandbox environment to test vulnerabilities and security mitigation strategies safely.

**3. Alignment Between Work and Major** The program directly aligns with and enhances my Cybersecurity major. Instead of focusing solely on traditional infrastructure security, my time at FCAJ deepened my understanding of the Shared Responsibility Model, secure Serverless architecture deployment, and real-time security monitoring utilizing AWS WAF, GuardDuty, and custom SIEM integrations.

**4. Opportunities for Learning & Skill Development** FCAJ served as a major launchpad for refining my skills in designing optimized Cloud Security architectures and leveraging AI/ML for information security (such as endpoint malware analysis and Deepfake detection). Furthermore, delivering technical presentations in English significantly elevated my communication and agile teamwork capabilities under tight deadlines.

**5. Culture & Teamwork** Amazon's "Day 1" cultural philosophy is deeply embedded in the program: every intern embraces a strong sense of Ownership and actively tackles new engineering challenges. The cohesion within our cohort, especially the seamless collaboration during group capstone milestones (such as team *ILOVEAWS*), fostered a unified collective ready to troubleshoot night and day.

**6. Policies / benefits for interns**
Although the program operates as an unpaid training bootcamp model, the core value lies in the substantial non-monetary assets provided. The company granted full access to premium global learning paths and extensive AWS Credits to build secure sandbox environments. Furthermore, the policy regarding self-funded professional attire served as a practical lesson, encouraging me to actively invest in my personal branding and adapt to global corporate standards.

### Additional Questions

* **What were you most satisfied with during your internship?** The immense volume of production-ready knowledge and the opportunity to master world-class Cloud/AI architectures under expert guidance. These intangible values far outweigh any initial personal investments.
* **What do you think the company could improve for future interns?** The organization could consider sponsoring a portion of the uniform costs or providing exam vouchers for international certifications (such as AWS Security Specialty) to further incentivize future cohorts.
* **Would you recommend this internship to your peers? Why?** Yes. Despite requiring a high level of self-reliance in terms of both personal financing and intense self-study, it remains a premier testing ground for Cybersecurity students to adapt to real-world infrastructure pressures.

### Additional Questions

* **What were you most satisfied with during your internship?** The invaluable opportunity to design, build, and deploy a real-world, end-to-end cybersecurity monorepo utilizing world-class cloud services. Having the autonomy to iterate and re-architect cloud networks under the direct mentorship of senior industry experts provided deep, practical SecOps insights.

* **What do you think the company could improve for future interns?** The 12-week bootcamp schedule is highly intensive, packing a vast amount of Cloud and AI knowledge. Extending the final Capstone Project phase by 1–2 weeks would give teams more headroom to fully optimize their source code and conduct rigorous load and stress testing.

* **Would you recommend this internship to your peers? Why?** Absolutely (100%). This is one of the most practical and high-quality workforce bootcamps in Vietnam for IT and Cybersecurity students, effectively bridging the gap between university academic theory and global cloud/AI enterprise demands.
