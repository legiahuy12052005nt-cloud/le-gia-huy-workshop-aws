> This section reflects my personal experience and key takeaways from the First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ). I hope these insights help the team continue to refine and optimize the program for future cohorts.

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment** 
The work environment at the AWS Vietnam office is highly professional, dynamic, and collaborative. The open-space layout sparks creativity and creates an ideal setting for intense teamwork and technical problem-solving.

**2. Support from Mentors & Admin Team** 
The mentors (AWS Solutions Architects) are incredibly knowledgeable and dedicated. They truly helped shape our troubleshooting mindset, especially regarding AWS Well-Architected security principles. The admin team was also extremely responsive and supportive, providing us with generous AWS Credits so we could safely test vulnerabilities and security strategies in a sandbox environment.

**3. Alignment Between Work and Major** 
This bootcamp was a perfect match for my Cybersecurity major. Instead of just sticking to traditional infrastructure security, FCAJ gave me deep, hands-on exposure to the Shared Responsibility Model, secure Serverless architectures, and real-time monitoring tools like AWS WAF, GuardDuty, and custom SIEM integrations.

**4. Opportunities for Learning & Skill Development** 
FCAJ was an amazing launchpad for improving my skills in Cloud Security architecture and applying AI/ML to info-sec (like malware analysis and Deepfake detection). Furthermore, presenting technical topics in English really boosted my communication skills and taught me how to work effectively under tight deadlines.

**5. Culture & Teamwork** 
Amazon’s "Day 1" philosophy is truly alive here. Everyone takes strong ownership of their work and eagerly dives into new technical challenges. The teamwork, especially within our capstone group (Team ILOVEAWS), was fantastic—we were a unified collective ready to troubleshoot day and night.

**6. Policies & Benefits for Interns** 
Even though this is an unpaid bootcamp, the non-monetary benefits are massive. We gained full access to premium global learning resources and enough AWS Credits to build real-world secure environments. Having to invest in our own professional attire was also a great practical lesson in personal branding and adapting to a corporate setting.

---

### Additional Questions

* **What were you most satisfied with during your internship?** 
I am most satisfied with the sheer amount of practical, production-ready knowledge I gained. Having the freedom to design, build, and re-architect a real-world cybersecurity project using world-class cloud services—all under the guidance of senior industry experts—was an invaluable experience.

* **What do you think the company could improve for future interns?** 
The bootcamp schedule is very intensive. It would be great if the final Capstone Project phase could be extended by 1–2 weeks, giving teams more time to fully optimize their code and run stress tests. Additionally, offering some support for professional attire or providing certification exam vouchers (like the AWS Security Specialty) would be a fantastic incentive for future interns.

* **Would you recommend this internship to your peers? Why?** 
Absolutely (100%). Even though it requires a lot of self-study and personal investment, it is one of the best and most practical workforce bootcamps in Vietnam for IT and Cybersecurity students. It perfectly bridges the gap between academic theory and the demands of the global tech industry.

### Proposals & Future Aspirations

* **Experience Enhancement Proposal:** I suggest adding deep-dive sessions on advanced cloud security topics, such as securing DevSecOps CI/CD pipelines, to better support students who are passionate about information security.
* **Future Aspirations:** I am highly motivated to keep learning and contributing to the AWS ecosystem. My ultimate goal is to become a fully-fledged Cloud Security Engineer.
